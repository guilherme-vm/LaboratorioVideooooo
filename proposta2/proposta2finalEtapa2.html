"And what then is the written word?"

With the audio adaptation of William S. Burroughs' words from "The Electronic Revolution" and clips from Burroughs' Cut-up Films, I created an experimental montage.
Split in 3 stages, the video brings us an introductory part, where a question pops "And what then is the written word?" giving something to think about with the rest of the video; bringing color to the video begins the second part (a part of deconstruction, with the company of the rythmic audio), with written words, symbols, and windows for the rhytmic replication; following this rythm, the third segment can give a feel of hypnosis cutting between the "static TV glitch" type effect clips and the "zoetrope" shape like light object, ending with the same sentence as it started "In the beginning was the word, and the word was god"
